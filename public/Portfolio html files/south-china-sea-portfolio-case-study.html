Mixed Media · 2024

South China Sea

Key Insight_
“What ordinary people don't know is already shaping their lives.”
Problem_
What ordinary people don't know is already shaping their lives.

A large-scale mixed media installation exploring the South China Sea conflict through the lens of public naivety. Presented as a detective board, it connects geopolitics to lived experience through cyanotypes, photograms, coffee-aged maps, and original photography.

My Role_
Sole artist across concept, research, photography, darkroom work, and installation.
Solution_
A detective board that makes viewers discover the conflict instead of being told it.

The final installation combined original photography, cyanotypes, photograms, coffee-aged map prints, handwritten notes, and red string into a seven-foot visual system.

Pramit Ranjan
01
Research_

Close to home, under-discussed, and layered in tension.

Starting from the wars in Gaza and Ukraine, I narrowed the project to the South China Sea conflict, a crisis close to home but often absent from ordinary public conversation.

Research_
The public knows fragments, not the full system.

Research focused on how economic, territorial, and military pressures overlap, and how little of that complexity enters everyday understanding.

Challenge_
The work needed a structure that felt investigative, not decorative.

A detective board became the central metaphor: red threads, mapped clues, and layered evidence mirroring how the public tries, and often fails, to connect the dots.

Pramit Ranjan
02
Solution_

Physical processes made the conflict feel discovered rather than explained.

Cyanotypes introduced blue as a material link to the sea. Coffee aging gave archival maps a worn authority. Photograms stripped figures of context and identity, while the detective-board structure held the research together.

Pramit Ranjan
03
Solution_

The detective board let viewers uncover relationships rather than receive a lecture.

Original photography, cyanotypes, photograms, and red-thread mapping worked together as one visual language. The installation made the conflict legible by turning it into something viewers had to trace for themselves.

Pramit Ranjan
04
Outcome_

The detective board was the right call. The piece lived in visual discovery.

What worked best was making the audience trace relationships for themselves. If revisited, the next step would be adding a stronger sound or ambient layer so the work reaches beyond the visual.

Outcome_
The board made viewers discover connections rather than be told them.

That active reading process became central to the installation's effect and made the conflict feel less distant.

Next_
The next version should add sound and bodily atmosphere.

The visual system carried the project. A stronger ambient or sonic layer would make the tension more immersive.
