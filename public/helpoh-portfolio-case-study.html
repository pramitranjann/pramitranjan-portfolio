UX Design · 2024

HelpOH

Key Insight_
“Two very different users, one platform. The design had to serve both without compromising either.”
Problem_
A two-sided problem: unreliable help for users, unfair work for workers.

HelpOH connects users with vetted professionals for household services while giving workers legal protections, training, and consistent income. Designed for Malaysia’s rapidly growing economy.

My Role_
Sole designer across research, UX strategy, UI design, and prototyping.
Solution_
Search, book, done, with transparent pricing and no friction.

A streamlined booking platform with clear service categories, location, date and time selection, and pricing clarity built to create first-time confidence.

Process_

Two personas, one platform, designed to serve both sides equitably.

The challenge was designing for users who need reliable, affordable household help and workers who need fair pay, trust, and consistent opportunities. The product had to balance both without feeling split in half.

Research_
Reliability for users, security for workers.

Mapped two personas, Farah and Arif, to make sure the platform worked for both the customer booking help and the worker building a sustainable livelihood.

Challenge_
Booking flows had to feel transparent, fast, and familiar.

The interaction borrowed trust cues from Grab so the experience would feel recognisable in the Southeast Asian market while keeping everything on a single booking surface.

Pramit Ranjan

Solution_

Search, book, done, with transparent pricing and no friction.

A desktop platform with streamlined search-and-book, transparent pricing, time selection, and service categorisation. Clarity over density.

Outcome_
Designing for two very different users clarified what the product needed to prioritise.
Approach_
Trust cues, transparency, and a single-surface booking flow drove the interaction model.
Next_
The next step is a dedicated worker dashboard with job management and training access.
