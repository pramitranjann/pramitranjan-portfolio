UI/UX · 2024

Atom OS

Key Insight_
“The hardest part wasn’t adding features. It was deciding what to withhold.”
Problem_
Subtraction is a design decision.

Atom OS redesigns the smartphone experience by removing distractions and replacing them with four intentional widgets. The phone should feel deliberately limited, not broken, just focused.

My Role_
Sole designer across concept, UI design, and prototyping.
Solution_
Four widgets. One focus. No noise.

A stripped-back phone OS with screen-time visualisation, app usage breakdowns, and a toggleable Focus mode that blocks selected apps for user-defined periods.

Pramit Ranjan

Process_

The need came from exam season. Existing solutions were too rigid or too blunt.

The concept started from a personal need to make the phone feel less compulsive without turning it into something unusable. The design had to balance intention with utility.

Research_
The problem was not screen time alone, it was the quality of attention.

I mapped two user types: students under academic pressure and professionals trying to reduce compulsive social media habits without losing essential phone functions.

Challenge_
Deliberate limitation had to feel designed, not broken.

Early explorations focused on widget hierarchy and black-and-white UI treatment so the phone would feel quieter and less psychologically magnetic without becoming hostile to use.

Pramit Ranjan
02
Solution_

Four widgets. One focus. No noise.

A four-widget home screen with screen-time visualisation, app usage breakdowns, and a toggleable Focus mode that blocks selected apps for user-defined periods.

Outcome_
Atom OS taught me that subtraction itself is a design decision.
Approach_
Minimal micro-interactions and clear widget hierarchy kept the system intentional, not sterile.
Next_
The next step is onboarding that explains the philosophy before the product asks users to adapt.
